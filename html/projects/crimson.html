Crimson Dissent

A contemporary floor lamp that combines geometric structure with ambient lighting, creating a striking interplay of form and illumination in modern spaces

about the project.

This contemporary floor lamp merges geometric structure with innovative lighting design, drawing inspiration from the Museum of Design Atlanta’s visual brand language. With a striking red and black color scheme, the lamp features a dual-dome construction that creates a dynamic interplay of light and shadow, elevating both form and function.

The lamp’s unique design begins with light escaping from the opening at the top of the shade, while the remaining light bounces off the reflective top dome. This light is then directed onto the bottom dome, which is painted a bold red. The light that reflects off the red dome creates a gradient effect on the wall, where the lower portion retains the original warm light from the bulb, and the top portion transforms into a subtle red hue, adding depth and warmth to the space.

The sleek, cylindrical black base of the lamp anchors the sculptural form, while the geometric dual-dome construction reflects MODA’s minimalist aesthetic. The clean lines and balanced proportions give the lamp a modern, artistic presence that fits seamlessly into contemporary interiors.

Designed to be a statement piece, this lamp highlights the fusion of design and functionality. Its innovative approach to light distribution and the bold use of red and black create a visually captivating atmosphere, making it a perfect addition to modern living spaces or offices.
